e-FGIS
          The Enhanced Farmland Geographic Information System (eFGIS) was developed to provide a more efficient method of managing parcellary map data and its attributes.
e-FGIS
In today's high-tech world, information technology such as Geographic Information System provides simple solutions for decision making where the key is the collation of disparate data into a usable format.

Most of the National Irrigation Systems data is still in traditional forms, such as paper maps or mylar maps, with limited information required for operation and maintenance. As a result of discovering these discrepancies between the data in records and the actual area of each farmer, NIA, with the assistance of the Japan International Cooperation Agency (JICA), has begun the preparation of Parcellary maps using Geographic Information System (GIS) methods and the establishment of the FARMLAND Geographic Information System.

In this regard, NIA decided to carry on with the JICA-recommended initiative of replicating TCP 3 activities and distributing and installing the system to the other National Irrigation Systems.

PROJECT DESCRIPTION AND SCOPE

Nia Region 11 has eight (8) NIS under implementation of Farmland Geographic Information System (FGIS) namely Lasang RIS, Padada RIS, Batutu RIS, Libuganon RIS, Kipaliku RIS, Libuganon Left RIS, Mal RIS and Saug RIS and one for implementation this year 2023 which is the Lupon IS with a total budget of P               distributed to three activities such as gathering of data, validation and purchasing of I.T. equipments..
